**Imagining Recursive Cycles**

Imagine you're playing a game of telephone with a twist – instead of saying a phrase, you pass a puzzle piece to the next person, and they add to it, passing it back to you. This goes on until someone finally completes the puzzle, or it's solved by a clever shortcut. In programming, this is a concept called recursion, where a function keeps calling itself until it finds the solution or hits a stop condition, like a missing puzzle piece.
