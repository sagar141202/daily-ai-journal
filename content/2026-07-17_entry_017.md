The title is like a variable - it's a label that holds information, but it's not the information itself. You can change the title to something new, and the book's content remains the same.

In the world of programming, variables are like containers that store values, making it easy to reuse them throughout your code. Think of it like a recipe book: by keeping frequently used ingredients in labeled jars, you save time and reduce clutter.

**Functions** are like secret agents - they take in information (called arguments), perform a mission (a task), and then disappear, leaving behind a result (a return value).
