**Galileo's Fateful Drop**
Legend has it that Galileo Galilei, the pioneering Italian physicist, was so convinced of his theory that objects fall at uniform rates, he deliberately dropped objects of different weights from the Leaning Tower of Pisa to demonstrate its truth. 

In 1599, while still a student, Galileo dropped two 6kg and 13kg stones from a height of 55 meters. His experiment, though initially conducted as a curiosity, laid the foundation for classical mechanics and cemented the fundamental principle of free fall.
