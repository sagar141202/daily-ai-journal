**Imagining Code as a City: Loops and Buildings**

 Picture a city where each building represents a line of code. Loops are like elevators - they take you up and down between floors, reusing the same path, without having to walk all the way back to the ground. In a program, loops help us execute a block of code repeatedly, making it easier to build more complex and efficient digital landscapes. Just like an elevator, loops save us time and effort, allowing us to focus on more creative endeavors.
