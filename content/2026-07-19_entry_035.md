**The Dance of Loops**
Imagine you're at a lively dance party, and you want to repeat a particular move – the electric slide – over and over. In programming, this repetition is achieved through loops, which act as a DJ who keeps playing your favorite song (the code) until you say stop. A "for loop" is like the DJ who prepares a set list, knowing exactly how many times to play the hit song.

**Looping Around**
Picture a hiker who wants to visit each of five scenic viewpoints. To get to each view without retracing steps, they create a mental map of their route, which is essentially a loop that returns to the starting point. In programming, this type of loop is called a "while loop," as it continues executing until a certain condition is met – much like the hiker reaching the next viewpoint.
